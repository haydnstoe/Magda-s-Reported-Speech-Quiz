Magda's Reported Speech Quiz

Level: Upper intermediate (B2)
Explanation

Reported speech (indirect speech) is used to tell someone what another person said without quoting them directly. Key changes when converting direct to reported speech: backshift of tense (present → past, past → past perfect), changes to modal verbs (can → could, will → would), changes to time and place expressions (now → then, today → that day, here → there), and pronoun adjustments for the new subject. The word "that" is optional in many reported constructions — you do not need to write "(that)" in your answers; either including or omitting the word "that" is accepted.

Direct: "I will finish the report tomorrow," she said. Reported: She said she would finish the report the next day.
Direct: "We have already sent the invoice," they told me. Reported: They told me they had already sent the invoice.
